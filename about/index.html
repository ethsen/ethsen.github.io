Who I am

A short narrative about my work, my interests, and the things that keep me curious.

I am a robotics graduate student working on learning systems that connect perception to action. My work tends to sit at the boundary between computer vision, decision-making, and real-world deployment. I like building projects that are clear to explain, easy to reproduce, and useful beyond a single demo.

Interests

I am most excited by problems that mix spatial reasoning with learning: multi-agent modeling, mapping, and perception systems that need to work outside pristine lab settings. I also care about clean tooling, experiments that are easy to repeat, and results that can be interpreted beyond accuracy numbers.

Beyond research

Soccer has been a constant in my life and it shows up in how I think about data, teamwork, and strategy. I like projects that blend technical depth with human context, whether that is building a tool for athletes, or exploring the way people interpret model outputs.
